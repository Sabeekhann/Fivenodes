What we believe

Our values aren't a poster

These four principles shape every line of code we write, every decision we make, and every engineer we hire.

Ownership

You own your work end-to-end. Not just the code that compiles, but the feature that ships, the performance that holds, and the bug that wakes someone up at 3am. No blame-shifting, no handoff excuses — if it's yours, you see it through.

Craft

We believe the quality of engineering is felt even when it's invisible. Clean abstractions, thoughtful APIs, code that the next engineer won't curse. We don't ship things we're embarrassed by — not because of pride, but because we care about the work itself.

Directness

We say what we mean. In code reviews, in client calls, in estimations. Polite vagueness wastes everyone's time. We prefer uncomfortable honesty over comfortable ambiguity — delivered with respect, but never watered down.

Growth

The best engineers we know are perpetually learning. We're working on hard problems at the frontier of AI and distributed systems — not CRUD apps. You should want to be a different engineer in 12 months than you are today. So do we.

Our process

How we hire

No whiteboard algorithms. No trick questions. We hire the way we'd want to be hired — direct, respectful, and focused on real work.

01

Initial conversation

30 minutes with Sabee. We want to understand what you've built, what you care about, and whether we're a fit — not your GPA or which companies you've interned at.

02

Technical assessment

A focused take-home challenge relevant to our actual stack. Designed to be completed in 3–4 hours. We review your architecture decisions as much as your code.

03

Paid trial project

One week, compensated at your rate. You work on a real (or anonymized) task from our pipeline. We see how you collaborate, communicate, and make decisions under real conditions.

04

Offer

If it's mutual, we move fast. Competitive compensation, clear expectations, and no surprises. We don't lowball and we don't play games.
